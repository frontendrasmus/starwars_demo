Kyoto

Kyoto's 1,200 years as Japan's imperial capital left an indelible mark on the city's identity—temple gardens, wooden machiya townhouses, and geisha traditions run through its neighborhoods like underground rivers. The city moves to the rhythms of seasonal change, from cherry blossoms engulfing the Philosopher's Path to crimson maples painting the hillsides each autumn. Here, ancient craft traditions and spiritual contemplation remain woven into daily life rather than cordoned off as museum pieces.

Quick facts

Population
About 1.47 million (within the city proper), but the larger metropolitan area has a population of over 2.4 million.
Area
Approximately 8.25 square kilometers (within the city proper).
Climate
Humid subtropical climate, characterized by four distinct seasons. Summers are hot and humid, and winters are cool.
Best season
Spring (especially late March to May) and Autumn (October to November) are highly recommended for cherry blossoms and vibrant fall foliage, respectively.
Currency
Japanese Yen (JPY).
Language
Japanese.

Kyoto was the imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years (from 794 to 1868), giving it an unparalleled concentration of traditional architecture, temples, and gardens.
