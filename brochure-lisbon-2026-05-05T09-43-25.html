Lisbon

Lisbon sprawls across seven hills where narrow cobblestone alleys connect sun-bleached plazas, while vintage trams rattle past azulejo-tiled buildings that have survived earthquakes and centuries of reinvention. The city balances fin-de-siècle elegance with gritty street art, saudade-soaked fado bars with contemporary galleries, and riverside warehouse districts with ancient castle ramparts. Here, pastéis de nata crumble in your hands as you watch the Tagus River catch the golden afternoon light from a neighborhood terrace where locals have nursed coffee for hours.
